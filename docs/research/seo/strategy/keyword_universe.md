Sample date: 2026-03-08

## Method

- Inputs used: current Monedario URL inventory (`/posts/`, `/calculadoras/`, `/guias/`, `/glosario/`, `/leyes/`), existing cluster structure in the repo, live public SERP sampling for representative Chile queries, and Chile-specific terminology used by AFC, ChileAtiende, INE, Banco Central, AFPs, CMF, and SII-adjacent result sets.
- Important constraint: this universe only maps indexable, meaningful targets. `/search/`, `/tags/`, tag detail pages, and paginated archives are intentionally excluded.
- SERP-feature notes: where Google modules such as featured snippets or PAA are mentioned elsewhere in the sprint, they are marked as direct observation or explicit inference. The browser tool exposed ranking pages reliably, but not every Google UI module directly.

## Highest-priority semantic conclusions

1. The strongest near-term demand/fit combination sits in `sueldo liquido y remuneraciones`, `impuestos personales`, `cesantia / AFC`, `UF / arriendo`, and the utility side of `APV`.
2. The biggest structural gap is not raw content quantity; it is cluster ownership. `empleo-ingresos` currently absorbs at least three distinct search-intent systems: sueldo/remuneraciones, cesantia/proteccion social, and presupuesto/control financiero.
3. `UF / IPC / arriendo` is split across current site areas, which weakens homepage routing, future hub design, and internal-link clarity.
4. Credit coverage is strong on diagnosis and renegotiation, but weak on two monetizable/high-intent explainer gaps: `prepago de credito` and `pago minimo tarjeta`.
5. `CAE` is currently a risky acronym because the repo already uses it for `Carga Anual Equivalente`, while many Chilean users mean `Credito con Aval del Estado`.
